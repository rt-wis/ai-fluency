Prompting Fundamentals

A good prompt has four ingredients

Think of it like briefing a highly capable new employee who knows nothing about your business — yet.

Context Task Format Constraints
C

Context

Who & Where

Tell the AI who you are, what industry you work in, and what situation you're dealing with. Without this, it writes for a generic audience.

T

Task

What to Do

Be precise about the action you want taken. "Help me with pricing" is a wish. "Write a 150-word justification for a 7% price increase" is a task.

F

Format

How to Present

Specify the output shape — bullet list, table, email, paragraph, numbered steps. If you don't specify, the AI guesses, and often guesses wrong.

C

Constraints

What to Avoid

Length limits, tone, things to include or exclude. "Keep it under 200 words", "don't be apologetic", "avoid jargon" — these dramatically shape the output.

See it applied — wire rope price increase

Context
You are assisting an Australian industrial distributor in the lifting and rigging sector. Our primary wire rope supplier is based in Europe (Germany).
Task
Write a professional justification for a 7% price increase on our 6x36 IWRC wire rope range, to be sent to a key customer.
Format
One paragraph of approximately 120 words — ready to paste into an email. No bullet points. No subject line needed.
Constraints
Reference AUD/EUR depreciation over the past 12–18 months, rising steel rod costs in European mills, and freight increases. Maintain a partnership tone. Do not be apologetic or overly formal. Avoid vague language like "due to market conditions".

What you get back

Following 18 months of sustained AUD/EUR depreciation — the Australian dollar has weakened approximately 9% against the Euro — alongside a 12–15% rise in wire rod input costs at European mills and meaningful freight increases from our logistics partners, we are implementing a 7% adjustment on our 6×36 IWRC wire rope range effective next quarter. This adjustment reflects the genuine cost movements in our supply chain, not a margin decision, and we remain committed to providing you with the consistent supply reliability and lead times you depend on. We appreciate your continued partnership and are happy to walk through the detail at your convenience.

The mental model: You wouldn't hand a new employee a two-word brief and expect a boardroom-ready output. AI is the same. The more specific your input, the more usable your output.
